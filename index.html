Apply for authority to trade prohibited weapons and ammunition

If you need to amend or renew an existing authority to trade prohibited weapons, do not use this application form.

To apply for the authority to trade prohibited (section 5) weapons and ammunition, you must have a valid registered firearms dealer (RFD) certificate.

Each company address where the prohibited weapons and ammunition will be stored, will require its own authority.

Each authority requires a named responsible person, who must hold a senior role within the company. We refer to this person as the dealer. A dealer's name can be used on multiple authorities. Each authority needs to be applied for separately.

Checks made by the police and HMRC

After the application is submitted, the Home Office will instruct the police to assess the dealer for suitability. The police will also carry out background checks on other members of staff where necessary.

The premises, storage facilities and the transporting arrangements will be assessed by the police, to ensure they meet the safety and security requirements.

Supporting evidence

A business case will need to be uploaded as part of the application. The business case must outline the background of the business and its objectives. It should also include evidence of any prospective trade, which should justify the number of weapons and ammunition requested for authorisation.

Before you start

You will need:

  • the type of company (limited, public limited, partnership or sole trader)
  • the company's name and Companies House registration number, if relevant
  • the RFD certificate number
  • the address of the premises to be authorised
  • the dealer's personal details (name, place and date of birth and nationality)
  • correspondence details (address, phone number and email address)
  • the types and numbers of prohibited weapons and ammunition requested for authorisation
  • a business case document

By submitting an application you agree to the police and HMRC carrying out their necessary checks.
