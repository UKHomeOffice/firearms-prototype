Apply for an authority to handle prohibited weapons and ammunition

If you need to amend or renew an existing authority, do not use this application form.

A Section 5 (S5) authority is required to acquire, possess, store, transport, manufacture, sell or transfer prohibited weapons or ammunition.

For a company to be considered for an S5 authority, it must submit evidence justifying the business's need for the authority, such as existing contracts or details of prospective business.

One or two senior members of staff must be named on the application. These individuals will be responsible for ensuring the company operates within the conditions of the authority.

Police will inspect the business premises to ensure they are suitable. The police will assess storage and transport arrangements where necessary.

The police will also interview the responsible individuals and may need to carry out background checks on other members of staff. HMRC will carry out a check on the company.

Before you start

As part of the application for an S5 authority, you will need to provide:

  • the company name
  • the personal details of the named responsible individuals (home address, place and date of birth, and nationality)
  • correspondence details (address, phone number and email address)
  • the address(es) where the weapons and ammunition will be stored
  • the types and numbers of prohibited weapons and ammunition requested for authorisation
  • justification for the S5 authority

By submitting an application you agree to the police and HMRC carrying out their necessary checks.
