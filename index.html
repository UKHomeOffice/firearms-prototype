Apply for authority to handle prohibited weapons and ammunition

A Section 5 (S5) authority is required to acquire, possess, store, transport, manufacture, sell or transfer prohibited weapons, component parts or ammunition.

Demonstrating a business need

For a company or sole trader to be considered for an S5 authority, evidence justifying the business need for the authority must be submitted. This may include existing contracts, details of prospective business or business plans.

Without evidence clearly justifying a business need, the authority won’t be approved.

Authority holder(s)

One or two senior members of staff must be named as the authority holders. The authority holder(s) are responsible for ensuring the company operates within the strict limitations described in the authority.

Police and HMRC checks

If sufficient evidence is provided for an application to be considered, the police will inspect the business and assess any storage and transport arrangements. The police will also interview the authority holders and may carry out background checks on other members of staff.

HMRC will carry out a check on the company.

Before you start

As part of the application, you will need to provide:

  • the company or sole trader’s name
  • details of how the weapons or ammunition will be obtained
  • details of what the weapons or ammunition will be used for
  • documental evidence showing a business need for an S5 authority
  • any address(es) where the weapons or ammunition will be stored, if necessary
  • the section types and quantities of weapons and ammunition to be covered by the authority
  • the details of the authority holders (home address, place and date of birth, and nationality)
  • correspondence details (name of contact, address, phone number and email address)

By submitting an application, you agree to the Home Office sharing information with the police and HMRC so they can carry out their necessary checks.
